Reasons to love Animal Crossing: New Horizons

Explore the Island

Players start their journey after purchasing a "Deserted Island Getaway Package" from the beloved raccoon entrepreneur, Tom Nook. Upon arrival, they receive a tent and basic tools, then begin the process of turning their island into a bustling village. The game offers full freedom to gather resources, craft furniture, decorate, and invite animal villagers to move in.

Over time, the island evolves into a community with stores like Nook’s Cranny, the Able Sisters’ tailor shop, and Blathers’ museum. Once players achieve a three-star island rating, musician K.K. Slider performs a special concert, considered the “end” of the main storyline—but there’s still so much to do afterward!

Express Yourself with Character and Clothing Customization

In Animal Crossing: New Horizons, you have full control over your character’s look. Hair, eyes, skin tone, and more can be changed at any time using a mirror or vanity, offering flexibility rarely seen in past games.

Clothing is a huge part of island life. With hundreds of tops, bottoms, dresses, shoes, and accessories available—plus new items daily at the Able Sisters’ shop—players can dress for any occasion. Custom designs allow for even more creativity, letting you wear your own art or share it with others. Whether casual or costume, fashion is one of the most fun ways to express yourself and connect with other players.

Interior Design: Happy Home Paradise

In 2021, Nintendo released a major paid expansion: Happy Home Paradise. This add-on lets players design dream vacation homes for villagers on a remote archipelago. Each villager comes with a unique theme or request, giving you creative prompts to build around—from cozy cafés to science labs to underwater lounges.

The expansion introduces a wide variety of new furniture, lighting options, room layouts, and partition walls. You can change ceiling decor, ambient sounds, and even adjust the time of day to match the vibe you're going for. These tools turn decorating into a true interior design experience, with nearly limitless possibilities. Best of all, many of the new features and items can be brought back to your main island, offering fresh inspiration for longtime players and a whole new reason to keep customizing.

The Museum: Collect and Curate

One of the most beloved features in Animal Crossing: New Horizons is the island museum, curated by the scholarly owl Blathers. From early in the game, players can begin donating bugs, fish, fossils, and artwork to build a beautiful, multi-wing institution that showcases the island's natural and cultural treasures. Each exhibit is presented in an immersive, thoughtfully designed space that rewards curiosity and attention to detail.

Bugs and fish change with the seasons, encouraging players to return throughout the year to complete their collections. Fossils can be unearthed daily and assembled into towering prehistoric skeletons, offering one of the most satisfying long-term goals in the game. The art gallery, introduced later, features real-world masterpieces—though players must be careful to spot clever forgeries when purchasing from the elusive Redd.

Whether you're a completionist or a casual player, filling out the museum offers a sense of quiet accomplishment and a lovely way to document your island life.
